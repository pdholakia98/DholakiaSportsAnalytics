Lamelo Ball: A Comparison

As of February 8, 2021, Lamelo Ball is the frontrunner for the NBA Rookie of the Year award. This post is meant to take a look at his current stats and compare to previous winners of the award.

Feb 8, 2021 • Parth Dholakia • 2 min read

fastpages   jupyter   sports   NBA   data   analytics   LaMelo Ball

Background and Approach

Lamelo Ball has been electrifying so far in his first year in the NBA, with some outstanding performances. Most notably, he put up 34 points against Utah on February 05. Vegas Insider has him as the current favorite to win the Rookie of the Year award (-150), which begs the question, how does he rank up to the previous Rookies of the Year?

To do so, we pulled the base stats of the previous rookies of the year and charted them against Lamelo to see where he ranks against the hottest rookies the NBA has seen. The below chart maps Lamelo's minutes per game against his points per game.

It's still about 23 games into the season, which explains why Lamelo's minutes are low compared to previous Rookies of the Year. His minutes are ticking up as he acclimates to the NBA schedule. He currently ranks as the 73rd in minutes per game, right next to Malcolm Brogdon in 2016-2017 and Mike Miller in 2000-2001. However, Lamelo is ranked 66th in points per game, also next to Brogdon and Miller. As LaMelo's minutes continue to increase throughout the season, I expect his points output to increase as well, due to the following chart:

LaMelo has a respectable 44% field goal percentage combined with a 34% 3 point percentage, which ranks him as 51st and 14th, respectively. However, we should take some of these numbers with a grain of salt as some of the great big men of the past didn't shoot any threes. If we take out the players who didn't shoot threes, Ben Simmons included, Lamelo ranks 23rd and 14th, respectively.

Given that LaMelo is a ball handler, it would be useful to see where he ranks in assists as well:

LaMelo is dishing about 6 assists per game, which ranks him as 15th on the list.

Summary

While still early in the season, LaMelo is showing great potential with his output as a rookie. While not putting up spectacular scoring numbers, his usage and assists indicate that he has a great opportunity to keep up his current pace for winning Rookie of the Year.
